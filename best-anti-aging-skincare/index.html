Anti-aging Series

When should you start using anti-aging skincare?

That’s a great question, and the answer you’ve probably heard before is that IT’S NEVER TOO EARLY TO START, in order to get healthy glowing skin.

However, the type of anti-aging routine you adopt should be chosen based on your age.

The best skincare for your teens and 20s

The mainstay of anti-aging here is “less is more”, with three easy steps:

1. Not over-cleansing. The aim of cleansing shouldn’t be to strip your skin completely of oil. You don’t want to destroy the skin’s natural skin barrier, which in turn results in premature signs of aging.

If you’re wearing makeup, it’s better to use two gentle facial cleansers (one oil-based and one water-based) rather than scrub at your skin with one harsh, oil-stripping cleanser.

Make sure to rinse off the cleanser within one minute, and remove it gently without scrubbing at your skin. Cleanse no more than twice a day.

The Heimish All Clean Balm, Farmacy Green Clean Balm, and COSRX Low pH Good Morning Gel Cleanser, are some of the best gentle cleansers to try. We also recommend the for makeup removal.

Make sure to choose a balanced pH cleanser. For more information about these types of cleansers, sign up to our monthly newsletter. We’ll also let you know when we discover newer cleansers with more organic ingredients.

2. Did you know UV radiation may be high even on cold and overcast days? UV causes wrinkling and pigmentation, as well as other signs of aging such as dry and sallow skin. For a breakdown of the best organic sunscreen to try, categorized by physical versus chemical sunscreens, see our post.

3. Always remove makeup and apply a hydrating serum or moisturizer at least once a day. We recommend <The Ordinary “Buffet” Hyaluronic Acid 2% + B5, and the Cosrx Advanced 92 All in One Cream as a lightweight moisturizer>. For dry skin, try the .

Skincare in your 30s

Now is the time to start introducing retinoids if you feel comfortable with it!

Retinoids are derivatives of vitamin A. There are many longitudinal controlled clinical studies in support of retinoid as an agent to increase cellular turnover, target pigmentation, and stimulate collagen synthesis.

Make sure you start with lower concentrations and work your way up, as retinol can be drying and irritating for the skin.

We recommend the best retinoid to start off with is the Ordinary Granactive Retinoid 2% In Squalane.

You can then try the Skinbetter Alpharet Overnight Cream or the Pestle & Mortar Superstar Night Oil if your skin is tolerating it, but make sure to only apply one retinoid product at any one time, and don’t use it together with any acids.

Just make sure you consult your doctor concerning use of topical retinoids during pregnancy (even though it’s more the oral retinoids that are contraindicated, we don’t recommend using topical retinoids during pregnancy or while trying to get pregnant, just to be safe).

If you haven’t started using eye cream yet, your 30s is the time to start! In our opinion, it’s never too early to introduce our top eye cream for the last 10 years, which is the Sulwhasoo Concentrated Ginseng Renewing Eye Cream. However, this can get a little exxy, in which case, explore the myriad of other top eye creams out there.

  • a drugstore eye cream like the Antipodes Kiwi Seed Oil Eye Cream
  • the affordable kbeauty eye creams by Innisfree Perfect 9 Repair Eye Cream and Innisfree Jeju Orchid Eye Cream. These are safe and moisturizing eye creams, but have relatively minimal anti-aging properties.
  • the Mizon Collagen Power Firming Eye Cream
  • the Elensilia Cpp Collagen 80 Intensive Eye Cream

To get recommendations about other top organic eye creams, subscribe to our montly newsletter.

It’s also really important to get on top of any age-related pigmentation that might pop up, like sun spots and solar lentigoes. See our breakdown of skincare to tackle pigmentation for the best organic products to use to tackle pigmentation.

Finally, make sure to exfoliate, but not over-exfoliate! Exfoliation helps increase cell turnover, and is therefore an easy way to get that healthy youthful skin glow. See our hyperpigmentation page for more exfoliator recommmendations.

This is a temporary solution though, so don’t get addicted to exfoliating your face everyday to maintain that glow.

It’s best to only exfoliate 1-2 times a week, or only apply exfoliation as a spot treatment to problem areas like scars and pigmentation (not active breakouts).

Skincare in your 40s

Firstly, you finally get to invest in formulations that contain higher amounts of more potent antioxidants, such as ginseng. We find the Sulwhasoo line perfect for this. Our top Sulwhasoo moisturizers are the:

  • Sulwhasoo Concentrated Ginseng Renewing Cream Ex Light (if you would like the versions with retinol, then choose the EX or original versions instead).
  • Sulwhasoo Hydro-Aid Moisturizing Soothing Cream
  • Sulwhasoo Essential Firming Cream

Secondly, you can also risk applying heavier lotions and creams, if you find your skin becoming drier. Our favourite moisturizers for dry skin include the Hada Labo Premium Lotion (consistency like a toner), Farmacy Sleep Tight Firming Night Balm, and the E-Nature Birch Juice Hydro Mist. Subscribe to our newsletter to get monthly updates on the best anti-ageing moisturizers to try.

Thirdly, start to layer two or three layers of product around your eyes - for instance both an eye serum and an eye cream.

Our top organic eye serum is the Moogoo Ascorbyl Tetraisopalmitate Vitamin C, which is good for lightening dark circles.

More best selling eye cream options include the Amore Pacific Intensive Vitalizing Eye Essence and the Drunk Elephant Shaba Complex Eye Serum.

Just make sure to keep Vitamin C products refrigerated in a dark container as it can become unstable in light. Also try not to use it with products containing niacinamide. Why? See our post on What Not To Mix.

The area around our eyes are some of the first to show signs of age, so it is important to target this area of your face as the focus of your anti-aging regimen.

You can also apply the leftover eye cream on your hands to any areas showing signs of age, such as the nasolabial folds (smile lines), forehead and neck.
