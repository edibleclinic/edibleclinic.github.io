Anti-aging Series

When should you start using anti-aging skincare?

That’s a great question, and the answer you’ve probably heard before is that IT’S NEVER TOO EARLY TO START, in order to get healthy glowing skin.

However, the type of anti-aging routine you adopt should be chosen based on your age.

The best skincare for your teens and 20s

The mainstay of anti-aging here is “less is more”, with three easy steps:

1. Not over-cleansing. The aim of cleansing shouldn’t be to strip your skin completely of oil. You don’t want to destroy the skin’s natural skin barrier, which in turn results in premature signs of aging.

If you’re wearing makeup, it’s better to use two gentle facial cleansers (one oil-based and one water-based) rather than scrub at your skin with one harsh, oil-stripping cleanser.

Make sure to rinse off the cleanser within one minute, and remove it gently without scrubbing at your skin. Cleanse no more than twice a day.

The Heimish All Clean Balm, Farmacy Green Clean Balm, and COSRX Low pH Good Morning Gel Cleanser, are some of the best gentle cleansers to try. We also recommend the for makeup removal.

Make sure to choose a balanced pH cleanser. For more information about these types of cleansers, sign up to our monthly newsletter. We’ll also let you know when we discover newer cleansers with more organic ingredients.

2. Did you know UV radiation may be high even on cold and overcast days? UV causes wrinkling and pigmentation, as well as other signs of aging such as dry and sallow skin. For a breakdown of the best organic sunscreen to try, categorized by physical versus chemical sunscreens, see our post.

3. Always remove makeup and apply a hydrating serum or moisturizer at least once a day. We recommend <The Ordinary “Buffet” Hyaluronic Acid 2% + B5, and the Cosrx Advanced 92 All in One Cream as a lightweight moisturizer>. For dry skin, try the .

Skincare in your 30s

Now is the time to start introducing retinoids if you feel comfortable with it!

Retinoids are derivatives of vitamin A. There are many longitudinal controlled clinical studies in support of retinoid as an agent to increase cellular turnover, target pigmentation, and stimulate collagen synthesis.

Make sure you start with lower concentrations and work your way up, as retinol can be drying and irritating for the skin.

We recommend the best retinoid to start off with is the Ordinary Granactive Retinoid 2% In Squalane.

You can then try the Skinbetter Alpharet Overnight Cream or the Pestle & Mortar Superstar Night Oil if your skin is tolerating it, but make sure to only apply one retinoid product at any one time, and don’t use it together with any acids.

Just make sure you consult your doctor concerning use of topical retinoids during pregnancy (even though it’s more the oral retinoids that are contraindicated, we don’t recommend using topical retinoids during pregnancy or while trying to get pregnant, just to be safe).

If you haven’t started using eye cream yet, your 30s is the time to start! In our opinion, it’s never too early to introduce our top eye cream for the last 10 years, which is the Sulwhasoo Concentrated Ginseng Renewing Eye Cream. However, this can get a little exxy, in which case, explore the myriad of other top eye creams out there.

  • a drugstore eye cream like the Antipodes Kiwi Seed Oil Eye Cream
  • the affordable kbeauty eye creams by Innisfree Perfect 9 Repair Eye Cream and Innisfree Jeju Orchid Eye Cream. These are safe and moisturizing eye creams, but have relatively minimal anti-aging properties.
  • the Mizon Collagen Power Firming Eye Cream
  • the Elensilia Cpp Collagen 80 Intensive Eye Cream

To get recommendations about other top organic eye creams, subscribe to our montly newsletter.

It’s also really important to get on top of any age-related pigmentation that might pop up, like sun spots and solar lentigoes. See our breakdown of skincare to tackle pigmentation for the best organic products to use to tackle pigmentation.

Finally, make sure to exfoliate, but not over-exfoliate! Exfoliation helps increase cell turnover, and is therefore an easy way to get that healthy youthful skin glow. See our hyperpigmentation page for more exfoliator recommmendations.

This is a temporary solution though, so don’t get addicted to exfoliating your face everyday to maintain that glow.

It’s best to only exfoliate 1-2 times a week, or only apply exfoliation as a spot treatment to problem areas like scars and pigmentation (not active breakouts).

Dealing with Breakouts

How to Get Clearer Skin and Prevent Breakouts

Whether it’s pesky pimples that pop up one after another, to full blown acne, there are some simple, non-medicated steps to control oily skin and help clear acne prone skin.

This is not a guide on how to cure acne, because there is technically no “cure”. Instead, the key is to find the best ways to calm down irritated skin and maintain clear skin over the long term; get rid of blackheads and whiteheads faster, and get rid of acne scars and fade post inflammatory hyperpigmentation.

The Best Cleansers for Breakouts

It’s tempting to reach for the strongest, most oil-stripping cleanser when you feel your pores are clogged and your skin is getting oily. But the aim of cleansing shouldn’t be to strip your skin completely of oil. Cleansing your face removes oil and sebum clogging your pores, but the resulting dryness can actually prompt over-secretion of oil.You also don’t want to destroy the skin’s natural skin barrier, which in turn results in premature signs of aging.

It’s better to use two gentle facial cleansers (one oil-based and one water-based) rather than scrub at your skin with one harsh cleanser.

Make sure to rinse off each cleanser within one minute, and remove it gently without scrubbing at your skin. Cleanse no more than twice a day.

I have combination skin, and personally use the Cetaphil Gentle Skin Cleanser because it’s the most effective cleanser I’ve found that does the best job at removing sebum (for all skin types) while not drying out the skin. However, it does contain parabens, so you can experiment with different cleansers to find one best suited to your skin type. If you are wearing makeup or heavy moisturizers before cleansing, it’s good to use an oil cleanser as a first cleanser, followed by a second gel based cleanser. The Heimish All Clean Balm and the Farmacy Green Clean Balm, are some of the best gentle oil-based cleansers to try. I’ve tried many other “cleaner” cleansing skincare products without parabens, but they either dehydrated my skin or left my skin feeling oily.

The COSRX Low pH Good Morning Gel Cleanser, works for some people, but can be too drying for others, especially if you lather up a foam with it. We recommend washing off this cleanser before it forms a foam. In general, be careful with foaming cleansers because they may be too drying for combination skin. Hyperpigmentation Series

How to get rid of acne scars and hyperpigmentation naturally

This is our suggested routine for fading blemishes, without using medicated treatments. It works for flat acne scars, post inflammatory hyperpigmentation, freckles and light coloured moles. Using natural formulations act slower than other therapies such as laser, so be patient. Remember to be consistent with this routine for 6 months to a year in order to see results.

Morning routine

  • Start with the COSRX Galactomyces 95 Tone Balancing Essence - this also contains niacinamide. For more information on the importance of niacinamide in your routine, see our post on dealing with breakouts. Make sure to pat this well into your skin to enhance absorption.
  • Then apply The Ordinary Niacinamide 10% + Zinc 1%. Niacinamide above 5% may be too irritating to use all over the face, so apply it only as a spot treatment to fade pigmented areas.
  • If you don’t want to use niacinamide exclusively, we recommend alternating The The Ordinary Niacinamide 10% + Zinc 1% with Cocokind Turmeric Spot Treatment. There is some evidence to suggest that turmeric can also aid in fading pigmentation.
  • As a last step, apply sunscreen of your choice, e.g. we recommend KraveBeauty Beet The Sun if you have dry skin. For our complete list of top sunscreens for different skin types, categorized by physical and chemical sunscreens, see our sunscreen breakdown post.

Night time routine

After cleansing, start with a chemical exfoliant. In our opinion, the best gentle exfoliant is the COSRX Aha Whitehead Power Liquid.

Despite the name, this is not so much for targeting whiteheads, but more as a general exfoliant.

COSRX also has a similar product called the Cosrx Mela White Ampule which also fades acne scar, but it a little pricier.

This contains an extra ingredient called Allantoin, which has moisturizing properties suitable for irritated, dry or scaly skin.

Other exfoliants to check out are The Ordinary Lactic Acid 10% + HA 2% and the KraveBeauty Kale-Lalu-yAHA.

Next apply The Ordinary Alpha Arbutin 2% + HA. This is a precursor of hydroxyquinone, so use this at night to be safe. Why? See our post on dealing with breakouts.

Finally, apply the Azclear Medicated Lotion 20% w/w. This is azelaic acid, which not only helps fade redness and pigmentation, but also has antibacterial properties. It comes as a pasty lotion that leaves a slight white cast, so it’s good to apply it before bed. If you prefer to try azelaic acid in a lotion form, check out the Finacea azelaic acid 15%.

Skincare Ingredients Decoded
What are the most popular skincare ingredients? This is the start of a series giving you the low-down on the key active ingredients popular in skincare today. Let’s start with niacinamide. Also known as 3-Pyridine Carboxamide, niacinamide is a form of Vitamin B3. There is no definitive clinical evidence for its efficacy as a topical ingredient in skincare. But there is some evidence to suggest an association between niacinamide and skin brightening, controlling hyperpigmentation, sebum control, rosacea and redness, inflammation, acne, fine lines, and anti-ageing in general. What Not to Mix
What are the skincare ingredients you should never mix? From our experience, L-ascorbic acid (the chemically active form of vitamin C) and niacinamide are a combination that not everyone knows to avoid. This combo is a bit controversial to begin with, because there are people who claim that combining the two are fine. What to watch out for is that it is quite easy to mix these two ingredients without even knowing it. [Read More]

What order should I apply my skincare?
Does the order I put on skincare matter? Firstly, as you probably know, you should layer your skincare from the thinnest to the thickest formulation. As a general rule, apply water-based formulations first, then lotions, then creams, then oils.
