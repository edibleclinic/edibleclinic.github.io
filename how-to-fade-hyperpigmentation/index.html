Hyperpigmentation Series

How to get rid of acne scars and hyperpigmentation naturally

This is our suggested routine for fading blemishes, without using medicated treatments. It works for flat acne scars, post inflammatory hyperpigmentation, freckles and light coloured moles. Using natural formulations act slower than other therapies such as laser, so be patient. Remember to be consistent with this routine for 6 months to a year in order to see results.

Morning routine

  • Start with the COSRX Galactomyces 95 Tone Balancing Essence - this also contains niacinamide. For more information on the importance of niacinamide in your routine, see our post on dealing with breakouts. Make sure to pat this well into your skin to enhance absorption.
  • Then apply The Ordinary Niacinamide 10% + Zinc 1%. Niacinamide above 5% may be too irritating to use all over the face, so apply it only as a spot treatment to fade pigmented areas.
  • If you don’t want to use niacinamide exclusively, we recommend alternating The The Ordinary Niacinamide 10% + Zinc 1% with Cocokind Turmeric Spot Treatment. There is some evidence to suggest that turmeric can also aid in fading pigmentation.
  • As a last step, apply sunscreen of your choice, e.g. we recommend KraveBeauty Beet The Sun if you have dry skin. For our complete list of top sunscreens for different skin types, categorized by physical and chemical sunscreens, see our sunscreen breakdown post.

Night time routine

After cleansing, start with a chemical exfoliant. In our opinion, the best gentle exfoliant is the COSRX Aha Whitehead Power Liquid.

Despite the name, this is not so much for targeting whiteheads, but more as a general exfoliant.

COSRX also has a similar product called the Cosrx Mela White Ampule which also fades acne scar, but it a little pricier.

This contains an extra ingredient called Allantoin, which has moisturizing properties suitable for irritated, dry or scaly skin.

Other exfoliants to check out are The Ordinary Lactic Acid 10% + HA 2% and the KraveBeauty Kale-Lalu-yAHA.

Next apply The Ordinary Alpha Arbutin 2% + HA. This is a precursor of hydroxyquinone, so use this at night to be safe. Why? See our post on dealing with breakouts.

Finally, apply the Azclear Medicated Lotion 20% w/w. This is azelaic acid, which not only helps fade redness and pigmentation, but also has antibacterial properties. It comes as a pasty lotion that leaves a slight white cast, so it’s good to apply it before bed. If you prefer to try azelaic acid in a lotion form, check out the Finacea azelaic acid 15%.
