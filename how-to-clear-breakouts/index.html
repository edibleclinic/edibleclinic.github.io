Dealing with Breakouts

How to Get Clearer Skin and Prevent Breakouts

Whether it’s pesky pimples that pop up one after another, to full blown acne, there are some simple, non-medicated steps to control oily skin and help clear acne prone skin.

This is not a guide on how to cure acne, because there is technically no “cure”. Instead, the key is to find the best ways to calm down irritated skin and maintain clear skin over the long term; get rid of blackheads and whiteheads faster, and get rid of acne scars and fade post inflammatory hyperpigmentation.

The Best Cleansers for Breakouts

It’s tempting to reach for the strongest, most oil-stripping cleanser when you feel your pores are clogged and your skin is getting oily. But the aim of cleansing shouldn’t be to strip your skin completely of oil. Cleansing your face removes oil and sebum clogging your pores, but the resulting dryness can actually prompt over-secretion of oil. You also don’t want to destroy the skin’s natural skin barrier, which in turn results in premature signs of aging.

It’s better to use two gentle facial cleansers (one oil-based and one water-based) rather than scrub at your skin with one harsh cleanser.

Make sure to rinse off each cleanser within one minute, and remove it gently without scrubbing at your skin. Cleanse no more than twice a day.

I have combination skin, and personally use the Cetaphil Gentle Skin Cleanser because it’s the most effective cleanser I’ve found that does the best job at removing sebum (for all skin types) while not drying out the skin. However, it does contain parabens, so you can experiment with different cleansers to find one best suited to your skin type. If you are wearing makeup or heavy moisturizers before cleansing, it’s good to use an oil cleanser as a first cleanser, followed by a second gel based cleanser. The Heimish All Clean Balm and the Farmacy Green Clean Balm, are some of the best gentle oil-based cleansers to try. I’ve tried many other “cleaner” cleansing skincare products without parabens, but they either dehydrated my skin or left my skin feeling oily.

The COSRX Low pH Good Morning Gel Cleanser, works for some people, but can be too drying for others, especially if you lather up a foam with it. We recommend washing off this cleanser before it forms a foam. In general, be careful with foaming cleansers because they may be too drying for combination skin. The Best Spot Treatment

Apart from using a cleanser, the best way to suck away sebum during the cleansing process is to use the Innisfree Jeju Volcanic Pore Clay Mask. Apply this only to the problem areas, leave on for a few minutes, then wash it off. For the best organic cleanser recommendations, see our anti-aging post, or subscribe to our newsletter.

To calm down your skin, first start with our favourite essence, the COSRX Galactomyces 95 Tone Balancing Essence, which contains galactomyces. Galactomyces is obtained from fermented sake, and is a great antioxidant, reduces sebum levels while still moisturizing your skin by promoting the skin cells to produce hyaluronic acid. Most importantly, galactomyces can be used by all skin types. This essence also contains 2% niacinamide, which is great for skin brightening.

An alternative to this is the Neogen Dermalogy Real Ferment Micro Essence, which contains saccharomyces ferment filtrate, as well as rice ferment filtrate.

If you can get away with wearing a thicker formulation over the top of this (such as dryer winter conditions or as part of your night time routine when you don’t need to worry about makeup sitting on top, or if you have very dry dehydrated skin to begin with), try the Etude House Soonjung Cica Balm Panthensoside 5.

The Best Pimple Patches

When you need to wear makeup over your breakouts, one favorite is the Cosrx Acne Pimple Master Patch. However, this usually works better if you apply it on pimples that have been “popped” (we recommend waiting for it to naturally pop).

A more expensive option is the Acropass Trouble Cure Acne Patch. This one is much more noticeable, so is best applied at night. It has the advantage of including tiny microstructures that can deliver active ingredients, including niacinamide and hyaluronic acid, into the pimple. It is suited for cystic acne in particular, but may not be suitable if you have had previous reactions to hyaluronic acid.

How to Get Rid of Blackheads and Minimize Pores

Our top two products to get rid of blackheads, are the COSRX Bha Blackhead Power Liquid (approximate BHA concentration of 2%) and the Glamglow Supermud Clearing Treatment. This COSRX Bha Blackhead Power Liquid is actually effective against both whiteheads and blackheads. It that does not work, try the Glamglow Supermud Clearing Treatment.

How to Fade Acne Scars

To fade acne scars, marks and blemishes, see our post on hyperpigmentation. The best gentle exfoliant is the COSRX Aha Whitehead Power Liquid. Despite the name, this is not so much for tackling whiteheads, but just as a gentle exfoliant.

How to Moisturize Acne Prone Skin

It seems counterintuitive to moisturize oily or acne prone skin, but believe it or not, breakouts are often the result of dry, dehydrated skin, which oversecretes sebum to compensate, which in turn clogs pores and causes breakouts. One of the top moisturizers to use for acne prone skin that is still dry is the Moogoo Full Cream Moisturiser. See our breakdown on the best organic moisturizers for more recommendations.

And always apply sunscreen! UV radiation actually enhances post inflammatory hyperpigmentation. The best sunscreens for oily skin include the Farmacy Green Defense Broad Spectrum Spf Mineral Sunscreen, and the Shiseido Urban Environment Extra Mild (this is a physical mineral sunscreen that can leave a white cast depending on your skin tone).
